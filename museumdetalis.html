Mysore Rail Museum

The Mysore Rail

Museum, established in 1979, is an outdoor museum showcasing vintage locomotives and other railway artifacts, offering a glimpse into the history of Indian railways. Located near the Mysore railway station, it's the second such museum in India, after the National Rail Museum in Delhi. The museum features a diverse collection, in-cluding royal coaches, steam loco-motives, and a gallery of photo-graphs and paintings depicting the evolution of Indian railways.

Key Features and Exhibits:

Royal Coaches:

The museum houses royal coaches used by the Maharaja of Mysore, including the "Maharani's Saloon" with a bedroom, dining car, and kitchen.

Steam Locomotives:

Various vintage steam locomotives, like the Wagnall & Co 119 E and the YP-2511, are on display, showcasing the evolution of train technology.

Austin Rail Motor Car:

A unique exhibit is the Austin rail motor car, a 1925 Austin converted to run on rails.

Photographic Gallery:

The Chamundi Gallery displays photographs and paintings illustrating the growth of Indian railways.

Miniature Train:

A battery-operated mini-train offers rides for children within the museum grounds.

Other Exhibits:

The museum also features railway signaling systems, telecommunication equipment, and other artifacts.

Visitor Information:

Timings: The museum is open from 9:30 AM to 5:30 PM on all days except Mondays.

Location:

It's situated on Krishna Raja Sagar Road, opposite the Central Food Technology and Research Institute (CFTRI).

Entry Fee:

Information on entry fees can be found at the museum's entrance or on the Mysore Tourism website.

Additional Information:

The museum provides a nostalgic journey through the history of Indian railways, with exhibits that appeal to both adults and children. It's well-maintained and offers a pleasant environment for exploring the railway heritage. The museum is a great place to learn about the evolution of trains, railway systems, and the role of railways in India's development.
