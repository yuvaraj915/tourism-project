Mysore Zoo

Mysore Zoo

officially known as Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens, is one of India's oldest and most popular zoos, established in 1892 by the then King of Mysore.

Located in the heart of Mysuru, it spans 157 acres and is known for its diverse collection of animals, in-cluding native and exotic species, and its focus on conservation and education.

Key Features:

History and Establishment:

Started as a private zoo by Chamaraja Wodeyar X, it was initially called Khas-Bangale and later became accessible to the public.

Animal Collection:

The zoo houses a wide variety of animals, including big cats (lions, tigers, jaguars), giraffes, zebras, elephants, rhinos, and various bird and reptile species. It is also the only zoo in India to exhibit a gorilla.

Conservation Efforts:

Mysore Zoo is involved in various conservation programs, including breeding endangered species and educating the public about wildlife conservation.

'

Visitor Amenities:

The zoo offers excellent visitor facilities, including well-maintained enclosures, a veterinary hospital, and a cafeteria.

Educational Programs

The zoo focuses on creating awareness about wildlife and conservation through educational programs and events, including an adoption program for animals.

Visitor Information:

Timings:

The zoo is open from 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM, except on Tuesdays, which is the weekly holiday.

Entry Fee:

Adult entry fee is ₹100, and for children aged 5-12, it is ₹50.

Location:

The zoo is located in Mysuru, approximately 2.5 km from the bus stand and 3.5 km from the railway station.

Best Time to Visit:

Early morning or late afternoon is recommended for viewing active animals, as many tend to rest during the hottest part of the day.

Adoption Program:

Visitors can contribute to the zoo's conservation efforts by adopting an animal and funding its caretaking expenses.
