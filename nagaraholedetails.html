Nagarahole National Park

Nagarahole National Park, also known as Rajiv Gandhi National Park, is a premier tiger reserve and wildlife sanctuary located in the Kodagu and Mysore districts of Karnataka, India. Established as a sanctuary in 1955 and later upgraded to a national park in 1988, it was designated as the 37th Tiger Reserve of India in 1999. The park is part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve and is known for its rich biodiversity, diverse flora and fauna, and scenic landscapes.

Location:

Situated in the Kodagu and Mysore districts of Karnataka, India.

Size: Spanning approximately 643 square kilometers.

Tiger Reserve: Declared the 37th Tiger Reserve of India in 1999.

Biodiversity:

Home to a wide array of wildlife, including tigers, elephants, leopards, gaurs (Indian bison), sloth bears, and various species of deer and birds.

Rivers: The Nagarahole and Kabini rivers flow through the park, enhancing its beauty and providing water sources.

Flora: The park features a mix of tropical deciduous forests, evergreen forests, and grasslands, supporting a rich variety of plant species.

Wildlife: Known for its healthy population of tigers, elephants, and other wildlife. It is also a birdwatcher's paradise with numerous avian species.

Activities: Offers jeep safaris, boat rides on the Kabini River, and guided nature walks for visitors to explore its natural beauty.

Accessibility: Well-connected by road from Mysore and Bangalore, making it easily accessible for tourists.

Conservation Efforts: Active conservation initiatives are in place to protect the park's biodiversity and promote sustainable tourism.

Elephant Congregation: Kabini backwaters attract large herds of elephants during summer. Part of Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve: Nagarhole, along with Bandipur, Mudumalai, and Wayanad, forms a large protected area in Southern India. @ Important Bird Area: The park is recognized as an Important Bird Area with over 270 bird species recorded.

Vegetation: Features moist deciduous forests with teak and rosewood trees.
Best Time to Visit: October to May (dry season) is ideal for wildlife viewing.

Mammals:

Bengal tiger, Indian elephant, Indian bison (gaur), leopard, sloth bear, four-horned antelope, Indian wild dog, sambar deer, chital (spotted deer), muntjac (barking deer), mouse deer, and more.

Birds:

Over 270 species, including Malabar trogon, Malabar pied hornbill, and the endangered Oriental white-backed vulture.

Reptiles and Insects:

Includes cobras, vipers, pythons, monitor lizards, and various species of butterflies and insects.

Diverse range of reptiles and insects also inhabit the park.

Visiting: Safaris: Jeep and boat safaris are available, offering opportunities to spot wildlife.
Guided Tours: Guided tours are conducted by trained naturalists to enhance the wildlife experience.
Accommodation: Resorts and lodges near the park offer safari bookings and transportation.
