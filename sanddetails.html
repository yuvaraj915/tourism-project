Sand Museum

The Mysore Sand Sculpture Museum is India's first sand sculpture museum, showcasing a collection of over 150 intricately crafted sand sculp-tures spread across 13,500 sq ft. Located on Chamundi Hills Road, it features a variety of themes, in-cluding Mysore's cultural heritage, wildlife, mythology, and more. The museum is open daily from 8:30 AM to 6:30 PM and is easily ac-cessible by road.

Key Details:

  • First of its kind: It is the first sand sculpture museum in India.
  • Artist: The museum was established by sand artist M.N. Gowri.
  • Sculptures: It houses over 150 sand sculptures, created using over 115 truckloads of sand.
  • Themes: Exhibits are categorized under various themes such as heritage, culture, wildlife, mythology, and fairy tales.
  • Location: The museum is situated on Chamundi Hills Road, about 4 km from Mysore city center.
  • Timings: Open daily from 8:30 AM to 6:30 PM.
  • Entry Fee: The entry fee is INR 60 per person
  • Time to Explore: Approximately 1 to 2 hours is recommended to explore the museum.
  • Nearby Attractions: Chamundi Hills is 7 km away, and other attractions like the Mysore Palace and the Zoo are nearby.
  • Preservation: The sculptures are protected by temporary roofs to shield them from rain.
  • Unique Experience: The museum offers a unique artistic experience with intricate sand carvings and diverse themes.
